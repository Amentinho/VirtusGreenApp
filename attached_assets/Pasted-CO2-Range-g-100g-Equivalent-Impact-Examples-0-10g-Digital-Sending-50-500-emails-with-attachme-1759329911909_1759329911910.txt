750 - 1,000g	• Cotton t-shirt: Manufacturing one complete cotton t-shirt<br>• Haircut: The total energy use of a hair salon per customer<br>• Mattress: About 1-1.3% of manufacturing a mattress<br>• Refrigerator: Running a fridge-freezer for 4-5 days
1,000 - 1,500g	• Laptop: About 1-1.5% of manufacturing a new laptop<br>• Restaurant meal: The kitchen energy + ingredients for 1-2 restaurant meals<br>• Leather shoes: About 7-10% of producing a pair of leather shoes<br>• Hotel night: About 8-12% of one night in an average hotel
1,500 - 2,000g	• Jeans: About 1.5-2% of producing one pair of jeans (full lifecycle)<br>• Furniture: Manufacturing a small wooden chair<br>• Gym membership: Energy use of a gym for one person for one month<br>• Vinyl record: Manufacturing 15-20 vinyl records
2,000 - 3,000g	• Running shoes: About 15-22% of producing one pair of running shoes<br>• Cotton sheets: Manufacturing one set of cotton bed sheets<br>• Washing: 30-45 loads of laundry in a washing machine<br>• Office work: The energy footprint of one person working in an office for 2-3 days

4,000 - 5,000g	• Smartphone: About 6-7% of manufacturing a new smartphone<br>• Wool sweater: Manufacturing one complete wool sweater<br>• Hotel stay: About one night in an average hotel (energy + services)<br>• Furniture: Manufacturing a small wooden table

6,500 - 8,000g	• Jeans: About 6-7% of one pair of jeans (from cotton field to store)<br>• Running shoes: About half a pair of running shoes (full production)<br>• Laptop: About 5-7% of manufacturing a new laptop<br>• Furniture: A medium-sized wooden bookshelf
8,000 - 10,000g	• Winter jacket: About 50-65% of producing a down winter jacket<br>• Handbag: Manufacturing one leather handbag<br>• Television: About 3-4% of manufacturing a 50" LED TV<br>• Carpet: Manufacturing 1-1.5 square meters of synthetic carpet<br>• Office chair: About half of manufacturing an office chair
